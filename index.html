FUN FACTS

Dissection

Andreas Vesalius was one of the first to dissect
human bodies. In doing so, Vesalius created some
of the first accurate illustrations of human anatomy.

Historical Significance

De Humani Corporis Fabrica
is regarded as the cornerstone of modern anatomy.
Vesalius accurately depicted the human body
nearly half of a millennium ago!

The Human Heart

Andreas Vesalius was the first to identify the two
chambers and atria of the human heart. This discovery
laid the foundation for our understanding of its functions.

Why Did I Choose This Book?

During our class visit to the Special Collections in Memorial Library, I was immediately in awe with De Humani Corporis Fabrica. The first thing to catch my attention was the size of the book (dimensions: 15 9/16 x 10 1/2 x 3 1/4 in). The book is set in pigskin and holds 700 pages. Even more fascinating to me were the illustrations. Within the book, there are many illustrations, such as the one above, which encompass the entire page. The details, such as the striations within the muscles, the bone structure, and the accurate depiction of organs blew me away. I could not believe that a book published over 500 years ago possessed such intricate and accurate detail, in addition to the fact that it has remained in overall good condition. After viewing several of Vesalius's illustrations, I knew that I needed to learn more.
